09Shadows

Soft, blue-tinted, multi-layer. Every shadow uses --color-shadow-tint (a navy ink) instead of black so elevation reads as part of the system rather than as photographic. Five neutral elevations, four semantic elevations for status surfaces, and six tinted focus rings.

Neutral elevation

--shadow-xsHairline lift — table cards, pills
--shadow-smDefault card / dropdown
--shadow-mdPopovers, hovered cards
--shadow-lgModals, sheets
--shadow-xlHero / floating notifications

Semantic elevation — for status surfaces

--shadow-success-sm / -mdConfirm toasts, success banners
--shadow-warning-sm / -mdPending dialogs, hold cards
--shadow-danger-sm / -mdError states, destructive prompts
--shadow-info-sm / -mdInformational cards

Inset & glass — surface treatments

--shadow-insetTop highlight + bottom shade — buttons, glass cards
--shadow-glassLight-glass elevation
--shadow-glass-darkDark-glass elevation — for inverse surfaces

Focus rings — accessibility

Every interactive control gets one of these rings on :focus-visible. The hue matches the action's semantic meaning so destructive vs. confirm vs. neutral actions feel different even before reading the label.
