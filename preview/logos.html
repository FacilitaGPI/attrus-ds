Design system

Logos & marks

The ATTRUS identity in its allowed forms. Two marks (wordmark + symbol), two contrasts (dark on light, light on dark) and a strict set of clear-space, background and proportion rules.

02Anatomy

Both marks are built around the same construction. The proportions are fixed — never recompose the parts.

apex glyph wordmark baseline
  1. Apex glyph The triangle / upward arrow abstracted from the first "A". It declares the brand even at favicon scale and works alone as the symbol asset.
  2. Wordmark The full word ATTRUS set in the custom geometric face. Optical kerning and tracking are pre-baked into the SVG — never re-set the type.
  3. Baseline The bottom edge of the wordmark. Use it to align the mark with other text or surfaces; the apex glyph reaches above the cap-height but never crosses below.
  4. Inner counter The negative space inside the apex glyph is part of the logo. Backgrounds with imagery or noise should never bleed into this space — it must stay clean.

05Clear space

Every mark needs breathing room. X = height of the apex glyph — that's the minimum padding on all four sides. Never less, ever.

X X X X

Practical rule: if any other element (text, button, image edge, photo) enters this padded box, the layout is breaking the brand. Crop the surrounding content or push the mark to a less crowded position.

08Don'ts

Six anti-patterns. Each one diminishes the mark — none of them belong in any ATTRUS surface, internal or external.

Don't stretch or squish. Always preserve aspect ratio. The wordmark's proportions are the brand.

Don't rotate. The mark always sits on its baseline. No tilts, even for stylistic effect.

Don't recolor. Only ink (#000033) on light and pure white on dark. No brand-blue wordmark, no rainbow.

Don't use the dark mark on mid-value backgrounds. If the background sits between neutral-300 and neutral-600, switch to a brand surface and use the light mark.

Don't place on busy photography. The mark needs a calm surface. If you must overlay imagery, add a brand-gradient panel or a glass layer first.

Don't combine the symbol and wordmark. The wordmark already contains the apex glyph. Stacking them is redundant and visually heavy.
