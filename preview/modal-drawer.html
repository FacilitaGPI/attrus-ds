Design system

Modal

The overlay system. Modals interrupt the user with a focused decision; drawers slide in from an edge for tasks that complement (rather than replace) the underlying view. Both share scrim, focus trap, and dismissal mechanics. Drawers now have a dedicated page with positions, platform rules and the sticky glass chrome.

01Which one?

Both interrupt the page with a scrim. The difference is intent: Modal is a focused decision, Drawer is a task panel.

02Anatomy

Match the surface to the shape of the data: scrim, surface, header, body, footer — modal sizes change the width only.

Archive counterparty?

Banco Itaú · BR · 4 active receivables

Archived counterparties stop appearing in transaction forms but their history stays intact. You can restore them anytime from Settings.

  1. ScrimFixed inset overlay, dims the page. Click closes the overlay (unless flagged blocking).
  2. SurfaceLight surface with large radius and 2xl shadow, centered in the viewport.
  3. HeaderTitle + optional subtitle + close button. Title uses sentence case, action-oriented.
  4. BodyScrollable region. Padding stays consistent with header/footer; content can grow beyond viewport.
  5. FooterTrailing-aligned actions. Primary action on the right, secondary/cancel to its left. Auxiliary info (counts, hints) can sit on the left with .modal-footer-spread.

03Modal sizes

Four widths. The body always scrolls if content exceeds viewport — never grow the modal taller than 100vh - 48px.

04Modal variants

Three flavors: default, destructive (with danger accent), and form. Variants change visual emphasis and footer composition — never the structure.

Title + body + 2 footer actions. The most common shape — confirmations, simple forms, info dialogs.
