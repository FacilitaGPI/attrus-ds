Design system

Cards

The container component. Cards group related content into a single visual surface — a record, a metric, an alert, a piece of UI machinery. They define where one thing ends and the next begins. Almost everything in the product lives in a card.

06Interactive cards

When the entire card is a click target — wrap it in <a> or <button> and add .interactive. Hover lifts; focus draws the standard ring; press settles back.

Triggers

  • :hover — border darkens, sm shadow, -1px translate
  • :focus-visible — 4px focus ring
  • :active — translate returns, shadow back to xs

Wiring

  • Use <a> with a real href
  • Or <button> for non-nav actions
  • Never wrap a card in onclick'd <div>

09Don'ts

Common pitfalls. Each undermines the contract that "card = single, self-contained unit of meaning."

MRRR$ 4.82M

Don't over-elevate stat cards. A KPI card never needs more than xs shadow. Heavy lift competes with real elevation hierarchy.

Sharp corners0px

Don't square-corner cards. The system minimum is --radius-2xs. Sharp corners feel jarring against the rest of the product's softness.

Custom colourOff-brand

Don't hex-pick a custom card surface. If the eight surface variants don't fit, propose a new one — don't override locally.

Click anywhere
(also click button)

Don't combine an interactive card with a button inside. Pick one — clickable card OR contained action. Both creates ambiguous click targets.

Nested card with shadow

Don't nest shadowed cards. Shadows compound and create visual noise. For nested groups, use .subtle or .flat.

Standard content

Not actually a warning

Don't use semantic accents for normal content. Semantic surfaces signal alerts. Using them as decoration cries wolf.
