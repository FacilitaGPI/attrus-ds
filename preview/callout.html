Design system

Callout

An icon-led note that sets a short message apart from running content — tips, warnings, results, and asides. Tonal variants carry meaning; the tint adapts to the theme.

01Tones

Five tones, each driven by two custom properties (--callout-tint for the box, --callout-fg for the icon). The background is color-mix'd against the surface, so every tone reskins automatically in dark mode — try the toggle, top-right.

03Anatomy

A leading icon (18px, or 16px when compact) and a .callout-body message. The body takes strong, code, and links; the icon is any Lucide glyph that fits the tone.

Structure

Markup
Defined once in components/callout.css and consumed by every doc page and product surface — edit the CSS and all callouts move together. See the catalog.
<!-- info (default) --> <div class="callout"> <i data-lucide="info"></i> <div class="callout-body">Your message, with <strong>emphasis</strong> or <code>tokens</code>.</div> </div> <!-- tonal + compact --> <div class="callout callout-warn callout-sm"> … </div>

04Rich content & actions

Wrap the message in .callout-content to stack a .callout-title, body and an .callout-actions row of buttons. Add a .callout-dismiss button to make it closeable. Buttons are the canonical .btn — use small ones (.btn-xs): .btn-secondary for the action (its border keeps it legible on the tinted box), .btn-ghost for the soft option, and .btn-danger for destructive.

05Alert bar

Add .callout-bar for a full-width banner: items center on one line, a left accent rule aids scanning, and actions/dismiss sit inline-right. Use at the top of a page or section for system-level messages. Add .callout-rounded when it sits inside a padded container rather than edge-to-edge.

06Inline & on-inverse

Two modifiers for tighter contexts. .callout-inline sizes the box to its content on a single line — for hero badges and inline notices, not full-width asides. .on-inverse swaps the light tint for a glassy translucent fill with light text, so a callout reads on a dark hero, photo, or inverse panel (mirrors .pill.on-inverse).

07Usage

A callout interrupts reading — spend it carefully.

Do & don't

Guidance
Do — match the tone to the message: warning for risk, success for confirmation, neutral for incidental notes. One idea per callout.
Don't — stack several callouts in a row, or use danger for anything that isn't an error. If everything is highlighted, nothing is.
For emphasis inside a sentence, use the accent text/highlight patterns instead — a callout is a block, not an inline mark.
