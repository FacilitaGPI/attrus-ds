Design system

Hero

The top-of-page focal element. Sets the identity of a screen — greets the user, announces the page, or anchors a marketing surface — with a brand-tinted backdrop, a confident headline and one or two clear actions.

01Anatomy

Six pieces stacked top-to-bottom. Eyebrow → title → sub → actions is the canonical order; tiles are optional and sit below.

  1. Backdrop A gradient (--grad-hero) plus a radial glow (--grad-radial-glow) for atmospheric depth. Never a solid colour — the depth is the point.
  2. Eyebrow Mono caps, --fs-xs, brand-muted. Sets context: company, breadcrumb, or release tag. Optional pill slot for status (v0.2, beta).
  3. Title Display weight (--w-black) at --fs-7xl with --ls-display. The largest type on the screen. One accent word may use --color-brand-secondary-200 for emphasis.
  4. Subtitle Plain body at --fs-md. Tells the user what the screen is or what is happening. Max ~56ch line length so multi-line stays readable.
  5. Actions One primary filled button (white pill on dark hero) and at most one ghost button. Never three. Skip both when the hero is decorative only.
  6. Optional KPI tiles A glass row below the actions — see §03. Use only on dashboard heroes.

06Accessibility

Heroes are decorative chrome but they also carry the page's primary heading. Treat them as semantic landmarks, not just visual flourishes.

ConcernRuleWhy
Heading level The hero title must be the route's <h1>. Eyebrow is plain text (not a heading). Screen readers announce the page name from <h1> — keeping the eyebrow as a styled <div> avoids polluting the document outline.
Contrast Title and sub use inverse-default and inverse-secondary tokens. Both pass AA against --grad-hero (tested at --color-brand-secondary-900 baseline). The radial glow doesn't change the worst-case contrast — the bottom-right corner stays dark enough.
Action targets Buttons are 40px tall, minimum 88px wide. --shadow-focus on :focus-visible. Meets WCAG 2.5.5 (target size). Ghost button keeps the white border at 22% opacity so the focus ring is visible.
Tiles Each tile gets role="group" with an aria-label describing the currency + amount + delta. The currency flag emoji is decorative; without aria-label, screen readers may skip the numeric value or read raw glyphs.
Backdrop motion No animation. The radial glow is static. Reduces visual noise and avoids prefers-reduced-motion friction.

07Don'ts

Five anti-patterns. All five appear in product designs from time to time. None of them belong in ATTRUS.

Pure black

No gradient, no glow

Don't use a flat colour. The hero gets its identity from --grad-hero + --grad-radial-glow. Without depth, it reads as a banner ad, not a hero.

Tiny title

Don't shrink the title. Heroes must use the display scale (--fs-7xl default, --fs-8xl marketing). Smaller titles look like cards, not heroes.

Page name

Title

One Two Three Four

Don't stack 3+ actions. Maximum one primary + one ghost. More buttons turn the hero into a toolbar — promote the extra actions to the page body.

Lorem ipsum

A very long, descriptive headline that wraps across several lines and keeps going just because it can

Don't write paragraph-length titles. Max 8 words, ~3 lines on desktop. Anything longer belongs in the sub, or split across multiple screens.
