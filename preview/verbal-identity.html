01Naming

The brand name is ATTRUS. Always uppercase. Never abbreviated. Never translated. Three rules that protect the mark from drift in copy, deck slides, contracts and emails.

ATTRUS
ATTRUS— correct form in any context, any language, any surface.
Attrus— never sentence-case.
attrus— never lowercase, even inside URLs or @-handles in body copy.
ATT · ATTR— never abbreviated.
Internal meaning

A unified ecosystem of financial services

ATTRUS represents the unique structure that connects markets, currencies and operations in a single platform. Used by team members, partners and regulators who already know the brand.

External meaning

Name + descriptive tagline

Externally, the name gains meaning when accompanied by clarity. That is why we always use a descriptive tagline to contextualise who we are and what we deliver — never the name alone in a first-touch surface.

02Tagline

The institutional endorsement. Appears with the logo on every brand surface that introduces ATTRUS to a new audience.

Institutional endorsement

Powering the flow of global financial services.

When to use
  • Always accompanying the logo on institutional pieces — covers, email signatures, OOH, decks.
  • Always in English — there is no official Portuguese version.
  • Can appear separated from the logo by a vertical rule when layout requires.
When not to use
  • On very small pieces where legibility would compromise hierarchy.
  • On low-contrast backgrounds or complex images.
  • Inside body copy as a quoted slogan — the tagline is a brand mark, not a quotable line.

03Hero lines

Five rhythmic phrases that translate the essence of ATTRUS into ready-to-use formats. Pulled into campaign pieces, landing-page heroes, OOH and event materials. Never paraphrased — pick one and use it as-is.

01One system. Built for your flow. Ready for every market.
02Unified structure. Local precision. Global confidence.
03Everything connected. Everything aligned. Everything simplified.
04Unified. Reliable. Adaptive.
05We structure the complexity. You focus on what moves your business.

Principles for creating new hero lines

  • Always in English. No Portuguese versions ship.
  • Short. Up to 8 words per sentence; rhythm matters more than density.
  • Reading rhythm. Short sentences, pauses marked by full stops, never by em-dashes.
  • Action or state verbs. operate · power · connect · structure · scale · route.
  • Avoid empty adjectives. incredible · revolutionary · unique — banned.

04Tone of voice

Close, institutional, technical, confident, consistent, direct. Each trait of the brand personality translates into a concrete language guideline.

01

Approachable

How it becomes language
  • Proximity pronouns (we · you · your company · your business).
  • Phrases with natural rhythm, as if in conversation.
  • Avoid robotic or generic tone.
Lexicon
side by side next to alongside as one on the same page in sync feel free to join us
02

Reliable

How it becomes language
  • Structural metaphors (bridge · foundation · direction).
  • Avoid exaggerations and superlatives.
  • Convey a sense of direction — every sentence points somewhere.
Lexicon
pillar backbone pathway track foundation keystone bridge compass
03

Adaptable

How it becomes language
  • Show understanding of different contexts.
  • Use analogies — make a complex flow legible.
  • Allow room for format variation across markets and audiences.
Lexicon
tailor tune adjust configure meet you where you are fit map to
04

Simplifying

How it becomes language
  • Objective sentences — one main idea per sentence.
  • Structures that guide reading and decision.
  • Eliminate redundancy and excess words.
  • Firm cadence — no excess connectives.
Lexicon
structure route flow engine control orchestrate
