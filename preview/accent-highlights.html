01The 3% rule

The brand book fixes the chromatic proportion across every piece. Navy and Black carry the weight; white gives air; Royal adds institutional energy; Sky is the 3% — always a highlight or an action, never a dominant background.

Usage proportion

From the brand book's chromatic system. The scarcer the colour, the louder it speaks — which is exactly why Sky must stay rare to keep its signal.

brand book · p.28
Navy40%
Black25%
White22%
Royal10%
3%
Navy + Black — 65%. Dominant on high-impact and institutional surfaces. The system's gravity.
White — 22%. Pause, breathing room, extended reading. The operational default for the platform.
Royal — 10%. More energy than Navy, still institutional. Solid fills, primary buttons, highlight blocks.
Sky — 3%. Action & emphasis only. The accent. Lose discipline here and the whole system flattens.

Why so strict? Accent works by contrast with restraint. If Sky appears on ten things per screen, none of them reads as "the action." Keeping it at ~3% is what lets a single Sky element pull the eye instantly — the entire point of an accent.

02Sky vs Royal — the two blues

The system runs on two blues that are easy to confuse. The rule is about weight vs energy: Royal is the institutional solid; Sky is the interactive spark. When in doubt, a surface or a button is Royal — Sky is only the thing you want clicked or noticed.

Royal · #003399 · brand-secondary

Royal — institutional weight

  • Solid primary buttons & their hovers
  • Filled controls: checkbox, radio, switch, slider
  • Highlight blocks, avatars, brand pills
  • Any solid fill that carries white text (10.86:1)
Sky · #3399FF · brand-accent

Sky — interactive spark

  • Selection indicators: active tab underline, selected row rail
  • Links & inline actions (as accent-700 on light)
  • Focus rings (--shadow-focus)
  • The single highlighted series in a chart

04Contrast & accessibility

Sky is a mid-tone — bright enough to fail white text and dark text alike at the 500 step. This single fact drives most of the role map above. Always step darker (accent-700+) when Sky must carry or be text on light.

Measured ratios

WCAG 2.1 — normal text needs 4.5:1, large/UI 3:1. Computed against the actual palette hexes.

accent-700 #1E6DC4 text on white Link 5.20:1 Passes — link / text-on-light
White text on accent-700 #1E6DC4 Step 3 5.20:1 Passes — Sky-family solid w/ white
White text on Royal #003399 Confirm 10.86:1 Passes — the solid-fill default

Audit fix applied. The stepper's current-step marker used a solid Sky-500 fill with a white number (2.94:1). It now uses accent-700 (5.20:1) — same Sky family, accessible. Everywhere else the system already steps to accent-700 for Sky-on-light text (active tabs, pagination) and uses Royal for solid white-bearing fills, so no other changes were needed.

06Don'ts

Four ways to spend the 3% badly. Each one either fails contrast or dilutes the accent until it stops meaning "action".

Don't put white text on Sky2.94:1 — fails AA. Solid action fills are Royal; if it must be Sky-family, step to accent-700.

Dashboard

Don't use Sky as a background"Never as the dominant background." Sky is a point of emphasis, not a surface.

Body copy set in Sky on white is hard to read at 2.94:1 and exhausts the eye over a paragraph.

Don't set body text in SkyRunning text is Navy/ink. Sky is for the occasional link, not paragraphs.

New 3 items Row

Don't sprinkle Sky everywhereFive Sky things and none reads as "the action." Pick one accent per view.
