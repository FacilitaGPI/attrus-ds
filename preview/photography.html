Design system

Photography

The direction for any photo that carries the ATTRUS brand — marketing pages, decks, OOH, social. Sober, focused, present. Never obvious stock or artificially happy scenes.

01Stance

Photography in ATTRUS supports — never decorates. Every shot must read as credible first and warm second.

Photography direction

Real people in professional contexts. Sobriety, focus, presence.

Never obvious stock. Never forced smiles. Never artificially happy scenes. ATTRUS speaks to financial operators, treasurers and compliance teams — people who recognise a stock image at first glance and dismiss the brand that uses one.

02Five principles

Five compositional rules every shot follows. Together they produce the institutional, restrained look that lets ATTRUS sit alongside regulators and banks without performing.

01

Composed posture

Thoughtful gaze, restrained gesture. Never forced smiles. The subject is engaged in something credible — not performing for the camera.

02

Corporate environments

Offices, meeting rooms, urban facades. Natural light when possible — large windows, daylight, soft shadows. Never lifestyle settings (cafés, sofas, kitchens).

03

On-brand tones

Blues, greys, neutrals. Cool side of the colour wheel. Avoid saturated warm colours — orange, red, yellow — unless they're an unavoidable detail in the environment.

04

Breathing composition

Negative space is part of the image. The subject occupies ⅓ of the frame; the rest is air. Crop tight only when the gesture is the whole point.

05

Authentic diversity

Different ages, ethnicities, genders, body types — without theatrics. The group looks like a real team, not a stock-library composite optimised for inclusion screenshots.

Colour-grading rules

  • Cast: very slight blue tint in shadows; neutral midtones; clean highlights. Never a warm orange/amber cast.
  • Saturation: reduced 10–20% from camera default. Skin tones stay natural; environment desaturates more than people.
  • Contrast: medium. Lift shadows slightly so detail survives on dark surfaces (decks, OOH). Don't crush blacks.
  • Forbidden: orange-and-teal Hollywood grade, vintage filters, vignettes, lens flares, heavy bloom.
  • Reference: the photography of Wall Street Journal long-form pieces — restrained, cool, observational.

04Composition

Three compositional patterns that recur across the brand. All three respect the rule-of-thirds grid; the subject sits on a thirds intersection, never dead-centre.

Right-third placement

Subject on the right vertical third, head at the top horizontal third. Negative space left for copy overlay.

Subject + environment

Subject occupies one third, environment (urban facade, office, infrastructure) fills the rest. Place tells story alongside person.

Centre-weighted group

Two or three figures, horizontally aligned, anchored at the lower-third line. Team shots, leadership rows, partnership announcements.

05On-brand vs off-brand

Side-by-side calibration. Same scenario, two treatments — only the left lands.

On-brand Cool-toned · negative space · single subject

Slate background, subject on the right third, restrained crop, no filters. Reads as institutional.

Off-brand Warm filter · centred · stickers · forced smile

Orange-and-yellow grade, subject dead-centre, theatrical smile, sticker overlay, vignette. Reads as marketing collateral, not infrastructure.

06What to avoid

Six patterns the brand book explicitly rules out. None of them belong on any ATTRUS surface.

Heavy artistic filters

Vintage tones, sepia, hard vignettes, lens-flares, faux-film grain. The brand reads as institutional — filters read as performance.

Artificially raised saturation

Punched-up reds, neons, oversaturated skies. Stay 10–20% under camera-default saturation.

Stickers & emojis on photos

The brand is institutional finance. Childish overlays — even on social — break the tone instantly.

Stock-library composites

Diversity panels arranged in front of a white wall, smiling at the camera in unison. Real teams don't pose like that.

Lifestyle / casual settings

Cafés, kitchens, sofas, parks. ATTRUS lives in offices, meeting rooms, urban facades, infrastructure environments.

Forced smiles

Wide, theatrical, camera-aware smiles. The brand subject is thinking, listening, deciding — never performing.
